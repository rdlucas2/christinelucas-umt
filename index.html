The method

What is Ultimate Music Theory?

Ultimate Music Theory (UMT) is a proven system that maximizes learning by engaging all three learning modalities (visual, auditory, & kinesthetic). Thoughtfully sequenced workbooks, paired with games and peer-group fun, make theory enjoyable — and applicable to the music you actually play.

See it

Color-coded charts, boxed key concepts, and flashcards make the page itself teach — visual learners thrive on every lesson.

Hear it

Concepts are spoken, sung, and discussed together in live classes — auditory learners lock it in by ear.

Do it

Write, move, and play — games and hands-on practice give kinesthetic learners the reps that make theory stick.

Each UMT workbook is a complete step-by-step course: 12 lessons, 12 review tests, a handy guide & chart, and 80 flashcards to dramatically increase retention.

Find your fit

Which level is right for me?

If you've never read music or are just starting an instrument, you're a beginner — start with Prep 1. If you study with a private teacher, they can tell you whether you've progressed to intermediate or advanced status.

Beginner

Prep 1 Rudiments

Perfect for beginning music readers.

  • The music alphabet
  • Reading on the treble and bass staves
  • Rhythmic notation, including duple 8th notes
  • Simple meters
  • Intervals up to the octave
  • Major/minor triads and pentascales

2026–27 sections: Mon 2:00 (virtual), Tue 11:00 (hybrid), Tue 3:00 (virtual), Tue 5:30 (in person)

Early intermediate

Prep 2 Rudiments

Great for early intermediate players.

  • The music alphabet and both staves
  • Rhythmic notation, including triplet 8th notes
  • Simple meters
  • Intervals up to the octave
  • Major & relative minor scales; circle of 5ths up to 2 sharps/flats
  • Major/minor triads

2026–27 sections: Mon 3:00 (virtual), Tue 2:00 (virtual)

Experienced · ≈ RCM Theory Level 5

Basic Rudiments

For experienced music readers comfortable in at least one clef and playing at a late intermediate level.

  • Major & relative minor scales; circle of 5ths up to 4 sharps/flats
  • All major and minor intervals up to the octave
  • Major and minor triads
  • Harmonic analysis with tonic (I/i), subdominant (IV/iv), and dominant (V)
  • Simple meters: duple, triple, and quadruple
  • Rhythmic notation, including triplet 16th notes

2026–27 sections: Tue 1:00 (virtual)

Meet your teacher

Christine Lucas

Christine Lucas is a pianist who currently wears many different musical hats: private piano teacher, music history professor, pit orchestra director, accompanist, church musician, and competition chair.

Originally from Center Township in Beaver County, she studied with Marina Lupinacci throughout high school. She attended Indiana University of Pennsylvania, earning a B.A. in Music Performance. During her junior year of undergraduate studies, she also participated in a semester-long student exchange at the University of Zagreb (Croatia) Academy of Music. She went on to earn an M.M. in Piano Performance with an emphasis in Piano Pedagogy from Florida State University, where she held a group piano teaching assistantship. She opened her private piano studio in 2006. She loves teaching students of all abilities, with a wide range of musical goals. Her students particularly look forward to her themed studio recitals. Several of her students have gone on to major in music and/or piano.

Beginning in 2012, Christine started teaching Introduction to Music as an online, 3-credit course at the Community College of Beaver County. This post allows her to share her love of classical music and history with students who may have never had any in-depth exposure to the genre.

Christine has a great love for ensemble playing and is proud to serve as the music director for Center Theatre Players, a non-profit, community musical theater group based in Rochester, PA. Her other local performances have included PMEA choral festivals, the Hopewell Community Big Band, and many high school musical pit orchestras. She served as music director of St. Joseph Parish in Coraopolis from 2017-2020 and remains an active substitute organist and cantor throughout the Pittsburgh area.

She coordinates the Lois Streator/Lois Smith Memorial Competition for the Greater Pittsburgh Chapter of Pennsylvania Music Teachers’ Association and is also on the board of the Steinway Society of Western Pennsylvania.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

What if I miss a class?

No problem. Classes are recorded on Muzie.live and saved so you can watch the replay and stay on track.

I've never studied music theory. Can I really start from zero?

Absolutely — Prep 1 Rudiments is designed for beginning music readers, including complete beginners. If you've never read music or are just starting an instrument, it's the perfect entry point.

What ages are the classes for?

Current course offerings are appropriate for ages 10 and up — and adults are especially welcome. Many of our students are choir members, community band players, and parents of young musicians.

What is Muzie.live, and what do I need to use it?

Muzie.live is an online classroom built specifically for music education. It's similar to Zoom, but the audio is ideally configured for musicians, shared materials stay available in one place, and classes can be recorded for replays. You just need a browser and a stable internet connection — no special software.

Is this the same as RCM theory?

The Ultimate Music Theory workbooks align with the Royal Conservatory of Music (RCM) theory curriculum. Basic Rudiments is comparable to RCM Theory Level 5, so UMT Club classes are a great foundation if RCM exams are in your future.

Do I need a piano or other instrument at home?

No instrument is required for class — everything we do works with your workbook and pencil. That said, connecting theory to an instrument you play makes it even more fun.

What happens at the end of the year?

Each level wraps up with a final exam — a chance to celebrate how much you've learned and confirm you're ready for the next level.
